Happy Me - Branding and Website

Happy Me was yet another educational platform, in which I was a part of the development of. Much different to the others, HappyMe was aimed at children with autism. That was something that made the whole process of designing for it, a whole lot harder.

We were faced with the challenge of creating something that had a friendly interface, is easy to use both by parents and children and follows all Aria principles. In the end, I designed a visual identity, a character and the whole website and assets for it.

research

My research consisted more of understanding autism, how children suffering perceive the world around them (sound, image, colour, speech), how I should design accordingly to the web Aria standarts. We got in touch with a mother of an autist child, who was out main tester and our main contributor to understanding the situation.

This helped me...

  • get in the shoes of a child with autism.
  • make the best design decisions, based on my research findings.
  • create a functional and aesthetically pleasing product.
