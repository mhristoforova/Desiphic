SoftUni Kids - Branding and Website

SoftUni Kids (actual website still has not launched) was created in order to introduce kids to the world of programming. The overall identity had to appeal both to children and parents, which made is a challenge to find the balance between the two age groups. My favourite part was designing the character, who is used across the platform. and in all materials.

This work involved user research, user flow, wireframes and visual mocks completed in a bit over two months. The outcome was a logo, printed materials, ads and a complete web platform. The website had to serve both an informational/promotional role, but also provide parents with the ability to manage and track their childrens' trainings and progress.

research

I started my research by conducting a survey with the upper target group of parents, seeing what works for them and what do they think the platform should consist of. As for the character, I showed the different sketches to kids and asked them which one would they pick out. I also researched other kids programming learning platforms and their brands, in order to be able to create something of the same level and style.

This helped me...

  • create a friendly and recognisable character
  • develop a platform which was easy to use even by parents, who aren't as advanced with computers and technology.
  • create an engaging brand
